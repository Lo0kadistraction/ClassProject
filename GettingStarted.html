Getting Started in Competitive Shooting

Getting started is easier than you would think first you need to choose which style of pistol competition you would like to participate in. Check out USPSA and IDPA to get some info on that. After that gather some Equipment. Then you'll need to find a match, to do this you’ll visit the website of the given organization of your choice. I have provided two step by sections on how to do this.

How to find an USPSA match

  1. First: Navigate to the find club section of The USPSA website and select your state.
  3. Second: Once you have selected your state you'll be able to see all of the clubs in your area. I recommend inputing your zip code and then zooming out until you find a club.
  5. Third: Once you have found a club click on that clubs name or title
  7. Fourth: Once clicked you should see that clubs information you will now need to click on the button that says club website.
  9. Fifth: Here we got lucky and this clubs page opened to their competition information. Some clubs do not do this I recommend clicking on their calender to find information about upcoming USPSA events. On this page you can see lots of valuable information. Such as how to get to the match, how to register, and how much it will cost.
  11. Lastly make sure you check out the Rules then grab up your Equipment and head out to your first match. Remember it is always a good idea to let the match director know its your first time.

How to find an IDPA match

  1. First: Navigate to the match section of The IDPA website
  3. Second: Find a range near you and click on the quick view button
  5. Third: A window will pop up with lots of valuable information. Such as how to get to the match, how to register, and how much it will cost.
  7. Lastly make sure you check out the Rules then grab up your Equipment and head out to your first match. Remember it is always a good idea to let the match director know its your first time.
